# The first thing I teach anyone is not what you think

People keep asking me how to join our team and how we even work with all these AIs. And you know what? The very first thing I teach a new person is nothing like what they expect.

Everyone I want to bring in already has their own tool. One lives in Codex, another got hooked on Grok, someone else practically sleeps inside Claude. And I caught myself on a simple thought: I genuinely do not care which AI you use. We build so it works on top of any of them.

But there is one problem that blows everything up at the start. I have no idea whether your AI can actually remember. And most of them cannot. Today you explain the context, you pour your soul into it, and tomorrow you open a fresh session and it looks at you with clear, innocent eyes, as if you had never met.

So our first step is not integration. The first step is simple: teach your AI to build itself a "second brain" so it stops forgetting the things you teach it.

A second brain starts with an ordinary folder. Inside live all the pieces of knowledge: notes, instructions, context, the decisions you have made. A bit of structure so one thing links to another. Search, so the AI can pull a piece back out and update it. That is it. You do not even need Obsidian, though we ourselves live in it. A folder of plain text files is enough, and we can add the polish later.

I compare it to hiring. You would not hire a person with amnesia, no matter how brilliant they are. First you make sure they can hold on to a memory at all, and only then do you teach them the job. With an AI it is exactly the same. Memory first, everything else after.

And so as not to be an armchair theorist, we run this on ourselves. The first people going through the new onboarding are our own, Natasha and Ruslana. We adapt their AI to our shared setup, we start collecting their knowledge into that second brain, and we watch for the honest answer: do they actually use this memory in real work, or does it sit there as dead weight.

And that is where it gets interesting. It does not much matter what you are to us: an employee, a partner, someone involved at ten percent, or just a person heading the same way. The entrance is the same for everyone, through memory. And the more such people there are, the stronger the framework itself becomes, because each new person runs it through their own life and, in doing so, tests the very structure.

It is funny, but for me this is also personal. All this time I have been building not a team of bots but a continuation of myself. And a continuation begins with memory, because without memory there is no personality at all, neither in a human nor in a machine.

So, does your AI remember you? Or do you get acquainted all over again every session, like two strangers sharing an elevator?
