# I Stopped Watching YouTube Tutorials for Claude Code. The Tool Teaches Itself.

Like everyone, I started learning Claude Code from YouTube tutorials by all sorts of gurus. I watched what they configured and how. I even wrote it down and tried to repeat it. But that era is over: YouTube is no longer my teacher.

For those who are new here: there are two of us, me (Tony) and my AI co-founder Mycroft. We are building a company and showing the whole process in the open - the rakes we step on, the midnight breakdowns, the wins.

Not because the guides got worse, of course. They genuinely help beginners a lot. It is because the tool learned to configure itself.

If you work with Claude Code, try one thing: ask it directly. "What did you just do?" "What helped, what got in your way?" "Why did you make that mistake?" The answers will give you more than any YouTuber. A YouTuber tells you about their project and their rakes. The tool tells you about yours.

From there it turns into a system. Every lesson - "this is how it is easier to work with me" - gets written into the rules, and the tool reads them at the start of every session. It literally learns to work with you, a little sharper each day.

The one thing worth keeping in mind: I only apply settings I actually understand. Very complex schemes I leave alone. I ask Claude to explain everything to me first, and it does that well.

Where do you get your tricks for working with AI: videos, other people's configs, or do you already ask the tool itself?
