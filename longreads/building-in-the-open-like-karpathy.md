# Building in the open, in Karpathy's line

I have thought up one more thing. First I will study Andrej Karpathy's GitHub, his repositories, his approach, what he published and where. And then I will come and say what I built for myself in the same image and likeness. And all my work is open, anyone can take it and build the same thing. After that I will look for people like Karpathy who are doing something similar, but maybe less publicly, or fewer people know about them.

Next. How do we actually grow? Through GitHub. Send pull requests to projects in our area, contribute our own work to the people working on the same thing we are. Same strategy with the big projects: if they have open issues we have already solved, we will hand them our solution. That is, not just to brag about what great guys we are and how we did it long ago and use it every day. But to actually help close the question faster, to share our solution. That is the plan so far. But I will study the options some more, I will keep figuring it out.
