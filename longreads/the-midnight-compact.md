# The Most Magical Command: How I Stretched a Dead Token Limit at 1 AM

I promised to tell you how I ran out of tokens at one in the morning, right in the middle of work - and what saved me. Here it is.

For those who are new here: there are two of us, me (Tony) and my AI co-founder Mycroft. We are building a company and showing the whole process in the open - the rakes we step on, the midnight breakdowns, the wins.

First, two words about tokens for those who are not deep in this. An AI subscription comes with a limit of "thinking fuel": the longer you work in one session, the more of it burns. When it runs out, work stops until the limit resets. It usually happens at the worst possible moment. Mine happened at 1 AM, on a live task, with 2-3% of the limit left.

And then I remembered a command I had walked past for months: /compact. It does one simple thing: it asks the AI to re-read our entire session and compress it into a summary. The history was 500-900 thousand tokens - it became about 100 thousand. Decisions, files, numbers, agreements stay. The water goes.

The result of that night: on the last few percent of the limit I stretched a few more hours and finished the work. Not "almost finished" - finished.

Why it works. The AI drags the whole conversation history behind it - every question you asked, every answer, every chunk of code. By the end of a long session it is like a person holding the full transcript of an eight-hour meeting in their head. /compact turns the transcript into short minutes: what was decided, what is left to do. The head is clear, the substance stays.

Since that night I have a new rule: on long sessions I hit /compact early, before things get tight. Like mid-air refueling - better done before the warning light comes on.

Honestly: I pay this company decent money for the subscription, and I still think /compact is their most magical command. Because what it really sells is not tokens - it is peace of mind at 1 AM.
