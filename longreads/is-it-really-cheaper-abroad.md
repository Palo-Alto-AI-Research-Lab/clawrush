# Is it really cheaper abroad? Let's actually count

People say too often that "living abroad is cheaper." Or at least I hear it a lot. Let's count.

The myth goes like this: somewhere in Vietnam or Brazil, for us Slavs, life is cheaper. Maybe. "Cheaper" is a relative word. I would take a closer look.

Food. Yes, local produce and fruit are cheaper. But the things you are used to, some Italian or Swiss cheese, imports, cost more.

Education. The local school is free. But put your child in an international one and you are already at roughly $15-30k a year per kid. And if you have several children, that is not a saving, that is a second mortgage.

Nanny. A local one might cost $500-1000 a month. But if you need a Russian-speaking nanny, it starts at $2k a month. It turns out the thing that matters to you is exactly the thing that costs more.

Housing, yes, often much cheaper, that is a real win. Cars, on the other hand, are often pricier.

The trick is that "cheaper there" is an illusion. Reality shows up when you count YOUR real expenses. And cutting them is not always possible. Your kids, their education, preserving your culture, your language, your habits, all of it matters, and you will not save much on it.

I have a big family, so I think about this a lot, I calculate, I compare. The conclusion I have reached is simple: the cost of a life is defined by what you are not willing to give up.

And how about you, those who have moved? Does it come out cheaper or more expensive overall? Which expense lines are the priciest? What were you not ready for?
