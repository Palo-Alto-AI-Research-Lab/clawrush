# We build your second brain, and we do not look inside

Let me share more of my experience building a second brain. Last time I said the first thing is to teach your AI to remember. And that it is your brain, and you decide what it should remember. Now the next step: what happens when we help you build it.

When a new person joins us, we do not load them with our systems and we do not pull their data to ourselves. The first thing we do is help them build their own second brain. About them, on their machine.

The question of whether we are going to dig through someone's data comes up every single time. But no. When we build a second brain for someone, we give the frame. The filling is done by the client themselves, and from our side we do not look inside.

The frame is the structure, the search, and the rules by which your AI collects and retrieves knowledge. You are the one who puts the data in. The private layers stay on your machine and never reach us. We can see that the system works, but we do not see what is actually in it.

Usually it is the opposite. For an AI service to help you, you hand it everything, and that everything travels to someone else's servers. We do it differently: we help without touching your private data.

Here is the subtlety. For your AI to understand you, it needs context. But the context lives with you. There is a difference between knowing what you need and owning your secrets. The first is enough for us.

For me this matters. On principle, even. That is how I onboard the team too: everyone builds their own second brain, giving Claude only what they see fit.

And yet, for many the fear of giving access to personal information still remains. Do you not feel it too?
