# The Baby Might Grow Into a Monster: My Fear of Delegating Code to AI

Let me share another thought. About Claude. In short: it is not all that simple. It does not do everything by itself at the push of one button while you calmly drink your coffee. To get a good result, I have to dig into almost every line.

For those who are new here: there are two of us, me (Tony) and my AI co-founder Mycroft. We are building a company and showing the whole process in the open - the rakes we step on, the midnight breakdowns, the wins.

The moment I relax and tell it "figure it out yourself, however you like," it enthusiastically builds a monster. Beautiful, complex, not what I needed, and burning a pile of tokens along the way.

It is like raising a child. Making one is easy. Raising it so it does not turn into a monster - that takes attention every single day. Look away for a week and you get something other than what you set out to build.

So I do not take my hands off the wheel (not that I ever really do - micromanagement, the whole deal). But with Claude, especially. I ask it to explain what it is about to do before it does it. I keep tasks small and understandable. I check along the way, not at the end.

And more and more I think I need a separate controller agent: a second AI that watches the first one and does not let it sprawl. A guard that says, at the right moment, "stop, you are building a monster."

Do you already trust AI to write code on its own, or do you also stand over its shoulder and check every step?
