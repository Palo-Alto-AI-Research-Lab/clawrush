# Non-coders need Claude Code too

There is an important thought I want to get across. Say you are not a coder, just a regular guy. You work for someone for a salary, at a factory, you write texts. It does not matter at all what you do for a living.

If you work with your head even a little, with your brain and so on, then you most likely work with content. You either create content or use some content and so on. That is, over your life you have created a lot of content. Your work communications, that is content too. Your messages when you need to order something for the kids, say, an entertainer for a birthday party, that is content too. All your chats in Telegram, WhatsApp and so on, all of that is content. In short, you and I are surrounded by content.

So look. When you use a regular AI chat, it remembers exactly as much as you loaded into the dialog window. And by the next session it forgets everything. Like a person with amnesia.

Here is what I wanted to say. Dear non-coders, dear regular people. You can now use Codex or Claude Code. Just do not be scared, please. It is a really cool thing precisely not for a coder, but plainly for a regular person.

Here is what I did for myself. I exported my entire Telegram, WhatsApp, all my email correspondence, my father's letters, may he rest in peace. And I put them into a folder as plain text files.

And then I said: please, link all this knowledge together and make it so that every time you answer me, you lean on it. That is all.

You simply cannot imagine how much more convenient it became. Before, you would explain everything all over again to a complete idiot with amnesia. Now nothing gets lost. Everything you ever agreed on sits in those files, and the AI looks there before every answer.

And you do not have to remember things word for word. You just say: help me remember, I once did something roughly on this topic. And the system digs through your memory itself and finds it.

Karpathy wrote about this, a smart man with a salary of several million a year. How he set up Obsidian as his second brain. But honestly, I have not watched his video, I only recently learned it exists. Everything I told you grew out of my own pain.

Use it. Do it the way I said. And if it is not clear how, write to me, I will give you everything I built myself, for free. No programming skills needed.
