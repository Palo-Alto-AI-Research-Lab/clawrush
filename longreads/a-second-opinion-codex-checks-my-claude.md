# A second opinion: my Codex checks my Claude before I act

We introduced one thing at our shop, we call it Second Opinion. A second opinion. My main agent is Claude, and the second opinion is Codex from ChatGPT. That kind of safety net. Now my AI goes and asks the other AI: am I being dumb here? This matters most with the ad account. Where there is money, where you can burn through it fast, I want two different brains to look at the decision, not one. We put this on everyone on the team. We made it a rule: use the second opinion always, constantly, on every action.

One thing to be clear about: the second AI's verdict is only advice, not an order. If my Claude thinks one thing and Codex thinks another, it does not stay quiet, it brings the human both opinions in plain words: I think this, Codex thinks that. The human decides. Why two at all. One model is confidently wrong and does not see it itself. The second one, from a different vendor, is wrong in a different way. Where the two disagree is exactly where a human should decide.
