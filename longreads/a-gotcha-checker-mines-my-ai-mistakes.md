# A gotcha-checker that mines my AI's own mistakes across every machine

Let me tell you about one thing I set up for myself. I call it the gotcha-checker. Gotchas, you get the idea, are our repeating screw-ups. The spots where my AI trips, walks in circles, and does not give me a result. And in every session those gotchas get logged.

Now I want to analyze the last hundred sessions across all my machines at once. Collect statistics: where, on which machines and in which parts, these screw-ups happen most often. Then understand the cause. Why does it even happen. Maybe our instructions file gets forgotten (I wrote about that recently). Maybe memory gets lost. Maybe a particular machine has its own quirks. And so on. And then I will try to remove these gotchas by finding the root. Like a doctor: do not treat the symptom, fix the cause. Although, with doctors, it is not that clear-cut.

I count 25 to 30 sessions per machine: the beacon, the hub, my colleagues' laptops, my own laptops and MacBooks. All of it has to be dug through to see where we keep stepping on the same rake.
