# I Am Not Afraid of the Machine. I Am Afraid of People.

Last time I admitted I was scared to let Claude write code on its own. That I keep my hands on the wheel, check every step, afraid it will grow me a monster while I sip my coffee.

Here is the thing. I dug deeper, and that is not my real fear. It is the convenient, presentable version of the fear. The true one is smaller and more embarrassing.

For the last few years I have barely earned anything. And I think I finally understand why. It is not the market. It is not the product I am building. It is me.

When something in front of me is hard, I run. And the hardest thing for me, it turns out, is not the code, not learning what a new model can do, not the latest release. The hardest thing is people. Selling. Walking out and saying, out loud, "look what I made, let us work together." Instead I go build the product a little cooler. One more feature and then, surely, I will be ready. That is procrastination, just dressed up as diligence.

The crack goes back a long way. When I parted with my previous co-founder, I lost the person who used to push me. Then I was seriously ill in 2021, and a lot inside me changed. I did not want to call, to meet, to sell. And do you know what I turned into? A policeman. I stopped going out to people myself, and instead I made sure everyone else did: calls by the book, a follow-up after each one, nobody late. I supervised other people's selling so I would not have to do my own.

Now, honestly, the part I am writing all of this for. I am ashamed to sell. Hard, blunt shame. Ashamed to announce myself loudly, to show off what I have done, to say out loud how much I have actually built and how much value my product carries. The cruel irony is that when I do finally start selling, I am good at it. My wall is not skill. My wall is starting.

But life rewards the people who do the opposite. Thousands of founders I have talked to know how to puff up beautifully and tell you a dazzling story, even when the product is half-baked, not even halfway done, or when there is no product at all and the idea itself is raw. Fake it till you make it. I still have to learn that.

So here is my concept now (or at least I will try, though I do not love that word): if I have a lemon, I should make lemonade. I stop hiding. Everything I do with my AI co-founder I bring out into the open, exactly like this. Not for a follower count, nobody is looking at that anymore. But so that these public posts push me out of my shell. Force me to communicate more, to handle objections, to clarify demand, to simply be on the radar. This "show" of mine is the act of selling myself. Exactly the thing I was afraid to do directly.

I always needed someone with a whip, someone standing over me who does not let me escape into endless development. Funny thing: now my AI is that partner. My machine does not let me hide.

And since I am done hiding, I will say the main thing out loud too, because staying quiet about it is the old me. I want to do one thing: bring engineers to these new tools, stand at that frontier. And if the road one day leads to a job at one of the companies that build these very models, I will be nothing but happy.

Tell me, is it the same for you? That I am not the only one with these thoughts and this state? And if you (or a friend of yours) have been through it, what exactly pushed you out into the open and made you act, made you show yourself?
