# My retro button: every session becomes memory

A neat little thing I came up with for myself. Maybe I am not the smartest guy in the village, but I invented a "retro" button. I just type /retro at the end of the work.

Remember I wrote that my AI lives in one continuous session and remembers what it was doing five minutes ago? Well, /retro is about what happens to that session when the work is done. So it does not get lost.

What the button does. It takes everything that happened in the session and makes a summary. It shows which questions are resolved, which ones are on me, and which ones are still left open. A very, very big summary.

And now the main part. That summary does not stay in the chat. It goes into my knowledge store. Into that same second brain I told you about, the one I am assembling my digital copy from.

Why I need this. A week later I will not remember what I was fixing and when, or who I promised to write a letter to. This way all my sessions sit in memory, and I can simply search through them.

And I can search not by exact words, but simply by meaning.

I do not know how you do it or how it is set up on your side. But this is what I made for myself. Every session now turns into a piece of memory instead of disappearing.
